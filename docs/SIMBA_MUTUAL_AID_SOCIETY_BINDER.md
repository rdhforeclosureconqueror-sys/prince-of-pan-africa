# Simba Mutual Aid Society Binder

**Status:** Core policy and system binder for Phase 1 documentation only.

## Purpose

The Mutual Aid Society is a future member-governed community care structure inside Simba. Its purpose is to prepare governed community support for urgent destabilizing needs after activation requirements are complete.

## Core Principle

Before commerce features, build the circle. Before money movement, build trust, records, roles, governance, accounting controls, privacy rules, and approval controls.

## Relationship to Simba Platform

The Mutual Aid Society is separate from STAR, Black Dollars, ownership contribution progress, partner reimbursements, and wallet behavior. Simba may later display status or support governed workflows only after the applicable phase is approved.

## Recommended Operating Structure

- Member assembly or governance body approves policy.
- Mutual Aid Committee reviews requests after activation.
- Treasurer or finance reviewer validates available funds and accounting controls.
- Privacy steward limits sensitive information exposure.
- Records keeper maintains minutes, decisions, recusals, and policy versions.

## Activation Rule

The Society remains **Building Toward Activation** until every activation requirement is satisfied and documented. No mutual aid distributions are authorized before activation.

## $20,000 Threshold

The designated fund must reach **$20,000** before activation. Reaching the threshold alone is not enough; governance, policy, accounting, privacy, and approval controls are also required.

## Funding Sources

Potential sources may include approved member dues allocation, designated community contributions, approved sponsorship support, grants, or other governance-approved sources. All source rules require accounting review before use.

## Member Rights and Limits

Members may receive clear information, respectful language, privacy protections, and access to approved procedures after activation. Members do not receive guaranteed aid, automatic approval, a cash-equivalent balance, or authority to direct fund movement.

## Aid Categories

Potential reviewed support categories include housing stabilization, utility shutoff prevention, emergency food support, transportation crisis support, urgent medical access support, family safety relocation support, bereavement emergency support, childcare emergency support, disaster response support, and other hardship categories approved by governance.

## Eligibility

Eligibility must be defined by approved policy. It may consider membership status, category fit, urgency, documentation, prior support history, available funds, conflicts, and committee review. Eligibility does not create guaranteed support.

## Governance Model

Governance approves policy, names roles, sets limits, receives aggregate reporting, reviews conflicts, controls policy changes, and confirms readiness before any launch. Committee authority is delegated and limited by written policy.

## Roles and Permissions

- Members: may read public status and later submit requests if approved.
- Intake reviewer: checks completeness.
- Committee reviewer: applies policy.
- Finance reviewer: verifies accounting controls and available funds.
- Privacy steward: monitors confidentiality.
- Governance liaison: reports aggregate status to governance.
- Administrator: manages records under approved access rules.

## Request Workflow

Future workflow: member reviews non-guarantee language, submits minimum necessary information, receives receipt confirmation, committee reviews, conflicts are handled, finance confirms controls, decision is communicated respectfully, and approved support follows manual controls approved by governance.

## Disbursement Rules

No disbursements occur before activation. After activation, support may move only after approval, documented controls, finance review, and governance-approved procedures. This PR authorizes no payment, payout, reimbursement, or runtime fund movement logic.

## Limits and Controls

Controls include category limits, per-request limits, monthly or quarterly fund limits, reviewer recusals, dual review, accounting verification, audit records, privacy limits, and governance reporting.

## Privacy Rules

Collect the minimum necessary information, restrict access by role, avoid public hardship details, use anonymized impact reporting, define retention, and document confidentiality expectations.

## Impact Reporting

Impact reporting should be aggregate and privacy-safe: total reviewed requests, categories, aggregate approved support, average response time, policy changes, and lessons learned.

## Data Model Draft

Draft entities: fund status snapshot, policy version, member eligibility reference, aid request, request document reference, review assignment, decision record, recusal record, accounting control checklist, manual disbursement record, appeal record, and aggregate report. These are planning concepts only.

## Platform Pages

Future pages may include a display-only overview, policy page, request status page, internal review queue, and reporting page. No pages are implemented in Phase 1.

## Public Language Guide

Use governed community support, reviewed support, community care, available funds, eligibility, documentation, approval, committee review, and impact reporting. Avoid language that implies automatic support, guaranteed aid, cash-equivalent member balances, or financial product behavior.

## Legal and Governance Open Questions

Open questions include legal structure, tax treatment, accounting classification, fund restrictions, privacy and retention requirements, member terms, appeal process, complaint process, audit process, public language review, and governance authority.

## One-Page Summary

The Simba Mutual Aid Society is Building Toward Activation. It may become a governed community support system after the $20,000 threshold is reached and after approved policy, governance process, accounting controls, privacy rules, and approval controls are in place. No mutual aid distributions occur before activation. Support is reviewed, limited by available funds, and not guaranteed. Phase 1 creates documentation only.

## Required Activation Guardrails

- Mutual Aid Society is currently **Building Toward Activation**.
- The **$20,000 threshold** must be reached before activation.
- Activation also requires approved policy, governance process, accounting controls, privacy rules, and approval controls.
- There are **no mutual aid distributions before activation**.
- Support is reviewed support, not guaranteed support.
- There is no automatic approval.
- There is no cash-equivalent member balance.
- There is no runtime fund movement in this Phase 1 documentation PR.
- There is no member-facing application flow yet.
- There is no payment, payout, or reimbursement logic in this PR.
