# Simba Cooperative Economy Binder

**Version:** 1.0 Planning Binder  
**Date:** 2026-06-24  
**Status:** Approved for planning / not approved for runtime launch  
**Purpose:** Define the Simba Cooperative Economy before wallet, partner, reimbursement, ownership, and mutual aid runtime workflows are implemented.

This binder is a planning document, not legal, tax, securities, banking, money-transmission, or cooperative governance advice. Before launch, this model should be reviewed by a cooperative attorney, securities attorney, tax professional, and payments/compliance professional.

---

## 1. Core Principle

**Every action strengthens the ecosystem.**

Simba is designed as a member-powered cooperative ecosystem where learning, service, spending, business participation, governance, and community development reinforce each other.

The system separates five different forms of value:

1. **STAR** — earned participation recognition.
2. **Black Dollars** — member-only community benefit credits usable inside the approved partner network.
3. **Ownership Contribution Balance** — recognized progress toward owner-member eligibility.
4. **Mutual Aid / Community Development Fund** — shared community capital built toward collective self-sufficiency.
5. **Partner Reimbursement** — controlled settlement to approved businesses that accept Black Dollars under agreement.

These lanes must stay separate in the system, in the database, in the user interface, and in public language.

## 2. System Philosophy

Simba is not just a website. It is a community operating system.

The long-term vision is to help members and Black-owned partner businesses coordinate learning, trade, ownership, preparedness, mutual aid, food systems, transportation, business support, education, and community development.

The platform should make visible that members strengthen the ecosystem by learning, sharing, supporting partner businesses, completing preparedness actions, earning STAR, and helping the community grow.

Partner businesses strengthen the ecosystem by accepting member benefits, serving members, paying partner dues, and participating in a verified trust network.

Owner-members strengthen the ecosystem by governing responsibly, staying active, and helping protect the cooperative mission.

## 3. Legal and Compliance Design Intent

The system should be designed around membership use, participation, cooperative governance, and community benefit, not passive investment or speculative profit.

A cooperative is generally owned and operated for the benefit of the people who use its services, and the SBA describes cooperative members as user-owners who may have voting power and may receive distributed earnings depending on the cooperative structure.

The ownership pathway should avoid being framed as a passive investment. The SEC's Howey framework focuses on whether there is an investment of money in a common enterprise with a reasonable expectation of profits from the efforts of others.

Black Dollars should be designed as a controlled, closed-loop member benefit credit, not open currency. FinCEN describes closed-loop prepaid access as value usable only for goods or services with a defined merchant, location, or set of locations, and references an exclusion for certain closed-loop prepaid access limited to $2,000 or less associated with a device or vehicle on any day.

Cooperative taxation, patronage dividends, nonprofit status, and tax-exempt status must be handled carefully. The IRS has separate forms and rules for cooperative associations and different categories of tax-exempt organizations.

### Compliance Guardrail

Nothing in the app should say or imply:

- Black Dollars are legal tender.
- Black Dollars are ownership.
- Black Dollars appreciate in value.
- Black Dollars can be traded outside the system.
- STAR is money.
- STAR can be purchased.
- STAR equals ownership.
- Ownership guarantees profit.
- Members are buying a passive investment.
- Businesses are cashing out an open currency.

Safer language:

- STAR recognizes participation.
- Black Dollars are member-only community benefit credits.
- Ownership Contributions track progress toward owner-member eligibility.
- Mutual Aid / Community Development Fund tracks collective community goals.
- Partner reimbursements are governed by partner agreements.
- Owner-member status requires participation, orientation, good standing, agreement, and governance approval.

## 4. Recommended Entity Structure

The cleanest legal and operational structure is likely a two-entity model.

### 4.1 Cooperative Operating Company

The cooperative operating company runs the member economy.

Responsibilities:

- membership dues;
- owner-member pathway;
- ownership contribution ledger;
- Black Dollar ledger;
- STAR ledger;
- partner business network;
- partner reimbursement program;
- partner business dues;
- platform operations;
- commerce coordination;
- cooperative governance;
- admin, verification, audit, and settlement tools.

Possible forms, depending on state and counsel:

- cooperative corporation;
- cooperative LLC;
- member-managed cooperative association;
- benefit corporation with cooperative bylaws;
- other state-specific cooperative structure.

### 4.2 Nonprofit Foundation or Community Association

The nonprofit or foundation supports the public-benefit side.

Responsibilities:

- education programs;
- cultural programming;
- youth programming;
- literacy and language;
- community preparedness education;
- grants and donations;
- scholarships;
- charitable relief;
- public-benefit research and curriculum.

This side should not be used to distribute private ownership benefits. It should have separate books, bank accounts, governance records, agreements, and compliance oversight.

### 4.3 Entity Separation Rule

The cooperative operating company and nonprofit/foundation must maintain separate bank accounts, ledgers, board minutes, contracts, tax records, and written service agreements when one entity supports the other.

## 5. Core Ledgers

### 5.1 STAR Ledger

Purpose: track participation, learning, service, contribution, and trust-building.

STAR is earned, not purchased.

Examples of STAR events:

- book read;
- audiobook listened to;
- language lesson completed;
- assessment completed;
- preparedness action completed;
- community resource shared;
- referral or invite action;
- business support action;
- volunteer review;
- verified service;
- builder contribution;
- event participation.

STAR should support member recognition, achievement badges, contribution history, trust signals, eligibility signals, community activity feed, and community progress goals.

STAR should not support direct purchase, cash redemption, ownership conversion, speculative value, person-to-person transfer, or outside trading.

### 5.2 Black Dollar Ledger

Purpose: track member-only community benefit credits.

Black Dollars are the credits that active members can use with approved Black-owned partner businesses inside the Simba / Assemble network.

Black Dollars may be used for discounts, perks, partial payment, approved member benefits, partner promotions, and community campaigns.

Black Dollars must not be used as ownership, cash, legal tender, investment, STAR replacement, open currency, outside-network payment, or person-to-person transfer unless approved later after legal review.

### 5.3 Ownership Contribution Ledger

Purpose: track recognized progress toward owner-member eligibility.

Recognized contribution sources may include:

- direct ownership contributions;
- eligible dues allocation;
- approved service credits;
- approved builder/operator credits;
- founder contribution credits.

Owner-member eligibility should require:

- required ownership contribution threshold, currently planned at $2,500;
- cooperative orientation;
- active good-standing status;
- signed owner-member agreement;
- governance approval;
- compliance review before runtime automation.

Reaching the dollar threshold should make the member eligible, not automatically owner-approved.

### 5.4 Mutual Aid / Community Development Fund Ledger

Purpose: track the shared fund that supports community development and mutual aid.

Planned rule:

- 50% of member dues may be allocated toward the Community Development / Mutual Aid Fund, if approved by governance and documented in member terms.

Activation rule:

- Mutual Aid Society activates when the community reaches $20,000 in the designated fund.

The fund should be visible on Member Home as a collective goal.

Display language:

**Community Mutual Aid Society**

50% of member dues are tracked toward a shared community mutual aid and development fund. The Mutual Aid Society activates when the community reaches $20,000.

Status: **Building Toward Activation**

### 5.5 Partner Reimbursement Ledger

Purpose: track what partner businesses are owed after accepting Black Dollars.

This ledger should record Black Dollars accepted, cash collected from the member, transaction verification status, reimbursement status, reimbursement amount, reimbursement method, settlement batch, payout date, dispute status, admin reviewer, and audit trail.

## 6. STAR System

### 6.1 Definition

STAR is the Simba participation and recognition system.

Member-facing copy:

**STAR measures contribution. Every book read, lesson completed, assessment taken, preparedness action finished, share made, and verified service act strengthens the ecosystem.**

### 6.2 STAR Principles

STAR should be earned, transparent, auditable, contribution-based, member-facing, community-building, protected from spam, and reviewed when high-value actions occur.

STAR should not be bought, sold, cashed out, traded, used as legal tender, or automatically converted into ownership.

### 6.3 STAR Categories

Recommended categories:

1. **Learn** — books, audiobooks, language lessons, curriculum progress.
2. **Reflect** — assessments, journals, growth reflections.
3. **Prepare** — household readiness, community preparedness, response training.
4. **Share** — verified sharing, invitations, resource distribution.
5. **Serve** — volunteer actions, contribution reviews, community service.
6. **Build** — approved builder/operator work, content support, system contributions.
7. **Support** — partner business support actions, receipts, reviews, recommendations.

### 6.4 STAR Integrity Rules

Duplicate or spam actions may not count. Some actions require verification. High-value actions require review. Admins can reverse fraudulent STAR. Members can appeal disputed STAR decisions. STAR policy changes must be announced. STAR should have an audit log.

## 7. Black Dollar System

### 7.1 Definition

Black Dollars are member-only community benefit credits.

Member-facing copy:

**Black Dollars circulate value inside the Simba partner network. Active members can use Black Dollars with approved Black-owned partner businesses for eligible discounts, perks, partial payment, or approved member benefits.**

### 7.2 Core Rules

Black Dollars:

- are available only to active members;
- can be used only with approved partner businesses;
- are usable only inside the Simba / Assemble network;
- are not cash;
- are not legal tender;
- are not ownership;
- are not STAR;
- are not publicly tradable;
- are not redeemable by members for cash;
- should not be transferable person-to-person in version one;
- may have usage caps and compliance limits;
- may be suspended for fraud or abuse.

### 7.3 Issuance Rule

Version one recommendation:

**Only the main Simba system issues Black Dollars.**

Partner businesses should not independently sell or issue Black Dollars at launch.

Reason: central issuance protects trust, simplifies accounting, reduces abuse, supports closed-loop controls, and lets the platform monitor caps, fraud, and outstanding liability.

### 7.4 Partner Business Acceptance

Partner businesses may accept Black Dollars only after verification, partner onboarding, partner agreement, business profile approval, settlement method setup, reimbursement rules accepted, fraud/dispute rules accepted, and privacy/community standards accepted.

### 7.5 Business Reimbursement

Partner businesses should not be described as “cashing out Black Dollars.”

Use this language:

**Partner Reimbursement Program**

Flow:

1. Member uses Black Dollars at an approved partner business.
2. Business records the transaction through the Partner Portal.
3. Member confirms the use.
4. Simba records the transaction.
5. The transaction enters pending verification.
6. Verification is completed by system checks, business confirmation, receipt upload, member confirmation, or admin review.
7. Approved transactions enter settlement.
8. Simba reimburses the partner under the Partner Agreement.

Reimbursement may be cash payout, platform credit, marketing credit, membership credit, community development credit, or hybrid settlement.

Version one recommendation: use controlled cash reimbursement from a designated reimbursement pool, if approved by counsel and accounting.

### 7.6 Black Dollar Value

Version one recommendation:

**1 Black Dollar equals $1 of approved member benefit value inside the Simba partner network.**

Suggested language:

**One Black Dollar may be applied as one dollar of approved benefit value with participating partner businesses, subject to program rules. Black Dollars are not legal tender, not cash, not ownership, and not redeemable for cash by members.**

Avoid saying the credit is backed by the U.S. dollar, stronger than the U.S. dollar, guaranteed exchange rate, investment value, or appreciating value.

### 7.7 Promotions and Multipliers

The base ledger should stay one-to-one.

Promotions can create temporary benefits, such as double Black Dollar value at selected farm partners, welcome credits, partner-sponsored discount campaigns, or community campaign bonuses.

Promotions should be recorded separately from base value.

## 8. Ownership Path

### 8.1 Definition

Ownership is a cooperative owner-member path, not a passive investment product.

Member-facing copy:

**Owner-member status is earned through commitment. Members may build toward owner-member eligibility through recognized ownership contributions, cooperative orientation, good-standing participation, and governance approval.**

### 8.2 Ownership Threshold

Initial planning threshold:

**$2,500 recognized ownership contribution**

Possible accelerated founder path:

**$5,000 recognized founder contribution**

These thresholds should be governance-approved and compliance-reviewed before public launch.

### 8.3 Recognized Contribution Sources

Recognized ownership contribution may include direct ownership contribution, eligible dues allocation, approved service credit, approved builder/operator credit, and founder contribution credit.

### 8.4 Owner-Member Eligibility Criteria

To become owner-eligible, a member must meet the required contribution threshold, complete cooperative orientation, stay active, remain in good standing, sign the owner-member agreement, pass compliance review if required, and receive governance approval.

### 8.5 Good Standing

Good standing may require active dues status, no unresolved fraud, no serious policy violations, no abuse of STAR or Black Dollars, no harmful conduct against the community, participation minimums if adopted by governance, and completion of required orientation or training.

### 8.6 Suspension and Removal

Ownership benefits, voting rights, and platform privileges may be suspended if a member is not in good standing.

Potential suspension reasons include fraud, chargebacks, abuse of STAR, abuse of Black Dollars, violation of cooperative agreement, harmful conduct, legal/compliance risk, non-payment, or inactivity if participation requirements exist.

Removal should require notice, reason, cure period where appropriate, review, decision, appeal option where appropriate, and written record.

### 9.1 Definition

The Mutual Aid / Community Development Fund is the shared fund that helps the community build financial capacity.

Member-facing copy:

**The Mutual Aid Society activates when the community reaches $20,000 in designated shared funding. Until then, the fund is building toward activation.**

### 9.2 Sources

Possible sources include designated member dues, business partner dues, donations to the nonprofit/foundation side where legally appropriate, sponsor contributions, community campaigns, grants, approved surplus allocation, and approved partner reimbursement reserve allocation.

### 9.3 Uses After Activation

Potential uses after governance approval include emergency member support, community preparedness, food systems, cooperative purchasing, business support, transportation, education, relief, verified mutual aid requests, and community infrastructure.

### 9.4 Controls

The fund should have a separate ledger, separate bank account or accounting class, governance policy, distribution rules, approval workflow, audit trail, member-facing summary, and privacy-protected reporting.

## 11. Black Dollar Transaction Flow

### 11.1 In-Person Transaction

1. Member visits approved partner business.
2. Business opens Partner Portal.
3. Business enters purchase total.
4. Business enters Black Dollars to apply.
5. System checks member active status, balance, daily limits, partner approval, eligible purchase category, and fraud flags.
6. Member confirms with QR code, PIN, or one-time code.
7. Business confirms sale.
8. System creates transaction record.
9. Transaction status becomes pending verification.
10. Verification completes.
11. Settlement is queued.
12. Partner is reimbursed under agreement.
13. Member receives receipt.
14. Admin audit log is updated.

### 11.2 Online Transaction

1. Member selects partner product or service.
2. Checkout shows eligible Black Dollar application.
3. Member chooses amount.
4. System checks limits and eligibility.
5. Member confirms.
6. Partner receives order notification.
7. Transaction enters pending or approved state depending on risk level.
8. Settlement occurs after fulfillment or verification.

### 11.3 Required Transaction Fields

Each transaction should record transaction ID, member ID, partner business ID, purchase amount, Black Dollars applied, cash amount paid, date/time, location or channel, purchase category, receipt reference, member confirmation method, business confirmation method, verification status, verifier ID if reviewed, reimbursement status, reimbursement amount, settlement batch ID, dispute status, reversal status, fraud flag, and audit log.

### 11.4 Transaction Statuses

Recommended statuses:

- draft
- pending_member_confirmation
- pending_business_confirmation
- pending_verification
- approved
- rejected
- disputed
- reversed
- settlement_pending
- settled
- flagged
- expired

## 12. Verification and Trust System

### 12.1 Purpose

Trust is the community's power.

Verification protects members, businesses, fund integrity, reimbursement pool, community credibility, and the partner network.

### 12.2 Verification Types

- automatic system checks;
- member confirmation;
- business confirmation;
- receipt verification;
- admin review;
- community verifier review;
- fraud risk review.

### 12.3 Community Verifiers

Approved verifiers may help review certain transactions or contributions.

Verifier requirements should include good-standing status, training, role permission, conflict-of-interest rules, audit logging, review limits, and appeal process.

### 12.4 Verification Outcomes

- approved;
- rejected;
- needs more information;
- escalated;
- reversed;
- fraud confirmed;
- duplicate detected.

### 12.5 Anti-Abuse Rules

The system should detect duplicate receipt uploads, repeated transactions at same amount/time, self-dealing, circular transactions, fake business accounts, fake member accounts, repeated refund abuse, suspicious high-volume use, chargebacks, and collusion.

## 21. One-Page Summary

## 24. Documents, Policies, and Controls Still Needed Before Launch

This binder defines the economic model, but it is not enough by itself to launch STAR, Black Dollars, partner reimbursements, ownership pathways, or mutual aid operations. Before runtime launch, Simba needs separate operating documents, legal review, governance approval, and implementation controls.

### 24.1 Required Operating Documents

Before Black Dollars, partner reimbursements, ownership contribution tracking, or mutual aid distributions go live, the following documents should be drafted and reviewed.

#### 1. Member Terms of Use

Purpose: define the rules every member accepts when using Simba.

Must cover account eligibility, member conduct, STAR earning rules, Black Dollar usage rules, wallet rules, member confirmations, transaction disputes, fraud and abuse, suspension and removal, role-based access, payment terms, privacy references, limitation of liability, policy change notices, and appeal rights where applicable.

#### 2. Black Dollar Program Terms

Purpose: define Black Dollars as member-only community benefit credits.

Must state clearly that Black Dollars are not cash, not legal tender, not ownership, not STAR, not redeemable by members for cash, usable only inside the approved Simba partner network, subject to limits and verification requirements, and may be suspended, reversed, or rejected for fraud, abuse, compliance risk, or policy violations.

#### 3. Partner Business Agreement

Purpose: define the relationship between Simba and approved partner businesses.

Must cover partner verification, business ownership/profile requirements, accepted purchase categories, Black Dollar acceptance rules, member confirmation process, receipt and documentation requirements, reimbursement method, settlement schedule, reimbursement limits, partner dues, tax responsibility, refund and chargeback handling, disputes, fraud and collusion prevention, data privacy, marketing use, suspension or termination, audit rights, and recordkeeping obligations.

#### 4. Owner-Member Agreement

Purpose: define the cooperative ownership path and owner-member obligations.

## 25. Governance Authority and Approval Matrix

This section defines who can approve, reject, pause, reverse, or escalate major actions inside the Simba Cooperative Economy.

The goal is to make sure no sensitive economic action happens without clear authority, records, and accountability.

### 25.1 Core Governance Principle

No single person should have unchecked control over money movement, ownership approval, Black Dollar issuance, partner reimbursement, mutual aid distributions, or policy changes.

Sensitive actions should require role-based permission, written policy, audit log, second approval, governance review, compliance review, member notice where appropriate, and appeal process where appropriate.

### 25.2 Authority Roles

The system should recognize Member, Paid Community Member, Owner-Eligible Member, Owner-Member, Community Verifier, Partner Business, Partner Business Admin, Operator, Admin, Superadmin, Governance Board or Cooperative Council, and Compliance Reviewer roles.

Members can earn STAR, use approved member benefits, view personal wallet records, confirm transactions, dispute transactions, and apply for ownership if eligible. Paid Community Members can access paid member benefits, use Black Dollars if active, participate in community goals, and build toward ownership if enrolled in the ownership path. Owner-Eligible Members have reached the required ownership contribution threshold but are not yet approved as owner-members. Owner-Members have completed the ownership process, signed the agreement, remained in good standing, and received governance approval.

Community Verifiers can review assigned transactions, receipts, contributions, or activity claims, but cannot approve payouts or change balances alone. Partner Businesses can accept Black Dollars only after approval, record transactions, upload receipts, view settlement status, and dispute reimbursement decisions. Partner Business Admins can manage partner users, submit transactions, view reports, and communicate with Simba operators.

Operators can review activity, support members, manage routine issues, prepare reports, and escalate sensitive items. Admins can manage users, partners, ledgers, disputes, reversals, limits, and review queues within approved policy. Superadmins can configure system-wide controls, emergency pauses, role permissions, and high-risk settings. The Governance Board or Cooperative Council approves major policy changes, owner-member status, mutual aid activation, large fund distributions, partner reimbursement rules, and economic model changes. Compliance Reviewers include attorneys, accountants, payments advisors, or approved compliance professionals who review high-risk changes before launch or expansion.

### 25.3 Approval Matrix

**STAR activity**

Members can earn STAR through approved actions. System can auto-award low-risk STAR. Community verifiers can review disputed or higher-value STAR. Admins can approve, reverse, or reject STAR events. Governance review is required before changing major STAR rules.

**Black Dollar issuance**

Only Simba may issue Black Dollars in version one. Admin can prepare issuance. Superadmin or governance approval should be required for large issuance. All issuance must be logged. No partner business may issue Black Dollars in version one.

**Black Dollar use**

Members can use Black Dollars only if active and in good standing. Partner businesses can accept Black Dollars only through the Partner Portal. System must check balance, limits, partner status, and fraud flags. Member confirmation is required. Partner confirmation is required.

**Black Dollar reversal**

Admin may reverse fraudulent or disputed transactions. High-value reversals should require second approval. Members and partners should have an appeal path.

**Partner reimbursement**

Operators may prepare reimbursement batches. Admins may review reimbursement batches. Superadmin or finance/governance approval should be required before payout. Every reimbursement must connect to verified transactions. No automatic settlement should occur in version one.

**Partner approval**

Operators may review partner applications. Admins may approve routine partner applications. Governance review should be required for high-risk, high-volume, or strategic partners. Partner suspension may be done by admin or superadmin. Partner removal should require documented review.

**Ownership contribution tracking**

Admins may record approved ownership contribution events. Service credits and founder credits should require governance approval. Dues allocation rules must be governance-approved before use. Ownership contribution records must be auditable.

**Owner-member approval**

Reaching the threshold only creates eligibility. Owner-member approval requires governance review. Approval must confirm contribution threshold, orientation completion, signed agreement, good standing, and compliance readiness. No automatic owner-member approval in version one.

**Ownership suspension**

Admin or superadmin may temporarily suspend benefits for urgent risk. Governance review should be required for long-term suspension. Removal requires notice, reason, review, decision, appeal option where appropriate, and written record.

**Mutual Aid Fund display**

Admins may update fund display only from verified ledger data. The fund may show progress before activation. The Mutual Aid Society must not appear active until activation requirements are met.

**Mutual Aid activation**

Activation requires confirmation that the $20,000 threshold has been reached. Activation also requires approved Mutual Aid Fund Policy, governance approval, accounting controls, and public member notice.

**Mutual Aid distribution**

No distributions before activation. After activation, distributions require application, review, approval, audit log, and conflict-of-interest screening. Large distributions should require governance approval.

**Policy changes**

Minor copy changes may be made by admins. Economic rule changes require governance approval. High-risk changes require compliance review, including Black Dollar transferability, partner-issued Black Dollars, cash redemption, automatic ownership approval, patronage dividends, mutual aid distribution rules, and reimbursement automation.

**Emergency pause**

Superadmin may pause Black Dollar usage, partner reimbursement, ownership processing, or mutual aid activity during suspected fraud, legal risk, technical failure, or security incident. Emergency pauses must be logged and reviewed.

### 25.4 Dual-Control Requirements

The following actions should require two-person approval or governance approval:

- large Black Dollar issuance;
- partner reimbursement batch release;
- ownership contribution correction;
- service credit approval;
- founder credit approval;
- owner-member approval;
- owner-member suspension beyond emergency hold;
- mutual aid activation;
- mutual aid distribution;
- partner removal;
- policy changes affecting money, ownership, or reimbursement;
- changing wallet limits;
- changing reimbursement rules;
- changing Black Dollar value rules.

### 25.5 Audit Log Requirements

Every sensitive action must create an audit log recording who performed the action, what action was taken, when it happened, affected member or partner, affected ledger, old value, new value, reason, policy reference, approval status, second approver if required, related transaction or document, and reversal status if later reversed. Audit logs should not be editable by normal admins.

### 25.6 Conflict-of-Interest Rules

A person should not approve a transaction, reimbursement, ownership credit, partner application, or mutual aid request if they personally benefit from it. Conflicts should be recorded, and a conflicted person should be removed from the approval path when possible.

### 25.7 Member Appeals

Members should be able to appeal STAR reversals, Black Dollar transaction rejection, account suspension, ownership eligibility denial, good-standing determination, and mutual aid denial if mutual aid is active. Appeals should include the decision being appealed, member explanation, supporting evidence, review timeline, reviewer identity or role, final decision, and written record.

### 25.8 Partner Appeals

Partner businesses should be able to appeal rejected Black Dollar transactions, reimbursement holds, reimbursement denials, fraud flags, suspensions, and removal from the partner network. Partner appeals should include receipt evidence, transaction records, member confirmation records, partner explanation, review decision, and final settlement outcome.

### 25.9 Launch Gate Authority

Before each phase launches, approvals should be recorded. STAR page launch requires product/admin approval. STAR ledger hardening requires admin and technical approval. Ownership contribution display requires governance and compliance review. Black Dollar balance display requires policy approval and member terms. Black Dollar issuance requires governance, accounting, and compliance review. Partner portal pilot requires partner agreement, admin controls, and pilot approval. Partner reimbursement requires accounting review, governance approval, and settlement controls. Mutual Aid activation requires fund threshold confirmation, fund policy approval, governance approval, accounting controls, and member notice. Ownership approval workflow requires owner-member agreement, governance approval, compliance review, and manual review process.

### 25.10 Section 25 Summary

The Simba Cooperative Economy must be governed by clear authority.

The system should not rely only on trust in people. It should create trust through roles, approvals, logs, policies, verifications, and appeals.

The operating rule is:

**No money movement, ownership decision, reimbursement, Black Dollar issuance, or mutual aid distribution should happen without authority, records, and review.**

## 26. Ledger Event Rules and Accounting Treatment

This section defines how STAR, Black Dollars, Ownership Contributions, Mutual Aid funds, partner reimbursements, and business dues should be recorded as ledger events.

The goal is to make every economic action traceable, auditable, reversible when needed, and separate from other forms of value.

### 26.1 Core Ledger Principle

Every balance shown in the system must come from ledger events. No wallet balance, STAR total, Black Dollar balance, ownership contribution balance, mutual aid fund total, or partner reimbursement amount should be manually changed without a recorded ledger event.

The operating rule is:

**Balances are not edited directly. Balances are calculated from approved ledger events.**

### 26.2 Separate Ledger Requirement

The system must keep separate ledgers for STAR activity, Black Dollar issuance and use, Ownership Contribution activity, Mutual Aid / Community Development Fund activity, Partner reimbursement activity, Business partner dues, Member dues allocation, and disputes, reversals, and adjustments. These ledgers may connect through references, but they must not be merged into one balance.

### 26.3 Ledger Event Structure

Every ledger event should include event ID, event type, affected member ID if applicable, affected partner business ID if applicable, affected fund or account, amount, unit type, source module, source transaction ID, status, created timestamp, effective timestamp, created by, approved by if required, reversal reference if reversed, policy reference, notes or reason, and metadata.

Recommended unit types include STAR, Black Dollars, USD, ownership contribution dollars, service credit dollars, reimbursement dollars, and mutual aid fund dollars.

### 26.4 Ledger Event Statuses

Recommended statuses include draft, pending, pending_verification, pending_approval, approved, rejected, reversed, disputed, settled, expired, cancelled, and flagged.

A pending event should not increase a final available balance unless the product intentionally shows a pending balance separately. Recommended wallet display includes available balance, pending balance, lifetime earned or contributed, and reversed or disputed amount if relevant.

### 26.5 STAR Ledger Events

STAR ledger events record participation and contribution.

Examples include book_read_completed, audiobook_listened, language_lesson_completed, assessment_completed, preparedness_action_completed, share_verified, referral_completed, business_support_verified, volunteer_review_completed, service_contribution_verified, builder_contribution_approved, event_attendance_verified, star_adjustment, and star_reversal.

STAR must be earned through approved actions. STAR cannot be purchased, sold, redeemed for cash, or directly converted into ownership. STAR can be reversed for fraud, duplicate activity, spam, or policy violation. High-value STAR events may require verification. STAR point values should be policy-based, not scattered through frontend code.

STAR should be treated as participation recognition, not cash, not liability, and not ownership. Final tax/accounting treatment must be reviewed by a professional before public launch.

### 26.6 Black Dollar Issuance Events

Black Dollar issuance events record the creation or assignment of Black Dollars to a member.

Examples include admin_issued_black_dollars, promotional_black_dollars, membership_bonus_black_dollars, partner_campaign_black_dollars, refund_replacement_black_dollars, adjustment_black_dollars, and reversal_black_dollars.

Only Simba may issue Black Dollars in version one. Partner businesses may not independently issue Black Dollars in version one. Every issuance must include reason, source, approver, and policy reference. Large issuance should require dual approval. Issuance should respect member balance caps and program limits and be visible in member wallet history.

Black Dollars may create a potential obligation or program liability depending on how the program is structured. Accounting treatment must be reviewed before launch. The system should track outstanding Black Dollars separately from STAR, ownership contributions, and mutual aid funds.

### 26.7 Black Dollar Usage Events

Black Dollar usage events record when a member applies Black Dollars at an approved partner business.

Examples include black_dollar_applied_to_purchase, black_dollar_purchase_pending, black_dollar_purchase_approved, black_dollar_purchase_rejected, black_dollar_purchase_reversed, black_dollar_refund, black_dollar_dispute_opened, and black_dollar_dispute_resolved.

Member must be active and in good standing. Partner business must be approved and active. Black Dollars must be used inside the approved network. System must check available balance, limits, and fraud flags. Member confirmation and partner confirmation are required. Transactions should remain pending until verification rules are satisfied. Approved usage reduces the member’s available Black Dollar balance. Reversed usage may restore Black Dollars if policy allows.

Black Dollar usage should never reduce ownership contribution balance, reduce STAR, or be described as spending cash.

### 26.8 Partner Reimbursement Events

Partner reimbursement events record amounts owed or paid to businesses after they accept Black Dollars under the Partner Agreement.

Examples include reimbursement_created, reimbursement_pending_review, reimbursement_approved, reimbursement_rejected, reimbursement_on_hold, reimbursement_scheduled, reimbursement_paid, reimbursement_reversed, reimbursement_disputed, and reimbursement_adjusted.

Reimbursement must reference verified Black Dollar transactions, follow the Partner Agreement, should not be automatic in version one, and batch release should require approval. Large reimbursement batches should require dual approval. Partner reimbursement should be paid from a designated reimbursement pool or approved operating account. Every reimbursement should link to settlement records and accounting exports.

Partner-facing language:

**Partner reimbursement is paid according to the Simba Partner Agreement after eligible Black Dollar transactions are verified and approved.**

Avoid saying cash out Black Dollars, exchange Black Dollars for cash, convert Black Dollars, or redeem Black Dollars.

### 26.9 Ownership Contribution Events

Ownership Contribution events record recognized progress toward owner-member eligibility.

Examples include direct_ownership_contribution, dues_allocation_to_ownership, approved_service_credit, approved_builder_credit, approved_operator_credit, founder_contribution_credit, ownership_adjustment, ownership_reversal, ownership_refund_or_return_if_allowed, and eligibility_threshold_reached.

Ownership Contribution Balance is separate from STAR and Black Dollars. Reaching the threshold creates eligibility only. Owner-member approval remains human-governed. Service credits, builder/operator credits, and founder credits require approval. Dues allocation rules must be approved before being applied. Ownership contribution events must include source, policy reference, and approval record.

Direct ownership contributions, dues allocations, service credits, and founder credits may have different tax and accounting treatment. A tax/accounting professional must review before launch. No public interface should imply guaranteed profit, appreciation, passive income, or automatic ownership approval.

### 26.10 Member Dues Allocation Events

Member dues may be split into multiple allocations if approved by governance and documented in member terms.

Example: a member pays $50 monthly dues, and the system may record $20 platform operations allocation, $15 Mutual Aid / Community Development Fund allocation, and $15 Ownership Contribution allocation if the member is enrolled in the ownership path.

Dues allocation event types include dues_payment_received, dues_platform_allocation, dues_mutual_aid_allocation, dues_ownership_allocation, dues_refund, dues_chargeback, and dues_allocation_reversal.

Allocation rules must be clear before payment, members should understand what portion goes where, allocation rules should be consistent with member terms, refunds and chargebacks must reverse the correct allocations, dues allocation toward ownership should not happen unless the member is enrolled in an approved ownership path, and dues allocation toward mutual aid should be reflected in the Mutual Aid / Community Development Fund ledger.

### 26.11 Mutual Aid / Community Development Fund Events

Mutual Aid / Community Development Fund events record collective fund activity.

Examples include member_dues_allocation_to_fund, business_dues_allocation_to_fund, sponsor_contribution_to_fund, donation_to_foundation_related_program, grant_allocation, surplus_allocation, fund_adjustment, fund_reversal, mutual_aid_activation_threshold_reached, mutual_aid_distribution_approved, and mutual_aid_distribution_paid.

Fund activity must be tracked separately from Black Dollars and Ownership Contributions. The fund should have a dedicated accounting class or separate account. Mutual Aid distributions must not occur before activation. Activation requires fund threshold confirmation, policy approval, governance approval, accounting controls, and member notice. Every distribution must be approved, logged, and tied to an eligible use.

Display rule: Member Home may show fund progress toward activation and should clearly say **Building Toward Activation** until all activation requirements are complete.

### 26.12 Business Partner Dues Events

Business partner dues may support the ecosystem, partner promotion, reimbursement reserve, platform operations, or community development depending on the partner agreement and governance policy.

Business dues event types include partner_dues_payment_received, partner_dues_platform_allocation, partner_dues_community_development_allocation, partner_dues_reimbursement_reserve_allocation, partner_dues_refund, partner_dues_chargeback, and partner_dues_reversal.

Partner dues must be documented in the Partner Business Agreement. Allocation must be clear. Partner dues should not automatically entitle a business to reimbursement beyond program rules. Partner dues should not allow a business to issue Black Dollars in version one. Partner dues may support reimbursement reserves only if approved by accounting and governance.

### 26.13 Reversals, Refunds, and Chargebacks

Every reversal should create a new ledger event. Original events should not be deleted.

Reversal event records should include original event ID, reversal reason, reversal amount, reversed by, approved by if required, member or partner notified, dispute reference, accounting impact, and timestamp.

Common reversal reasons include duplicate transaction, fraud, member dispute, partner dispute, chargeback, refund, mistaken entry, policy violation, compliance hold, and admin correction.

Refunds should reverse the original allocation path where possible. If Black Dollars were used, refund policy must define whether Black Dollars are restored, expired, or voided. If a partner was already reimbursed, the system must determine whether reimbursement is reversed, offset, or recovered. If dues were refunded, mutual aid and ownership allocations may need reversal. If STAR was earned from a refunded transaction, STAR may need reversal.

### 26.14 Dispute Ledger Events

Disputes must be tracked separately from the original transaction.

Dispute event types include dispute_opened, dispute_evidence_submitted, dispute_under_review, dispute_escalated, dispute_resolved_member_favor, dispute_resolved_partner_favor, dispute_resolved_platform_favor, dispute_closed, and dispute_reopened.

Disputes should freeze affected pending reimbursements when appropriate, not automatically delete member or partner records, record evidence, show review timeline, log final decisions, and reference appeals.

### 26.15 Accounting Export Requirements

The system should support accounting exports for member dues received, dues allocations, Black Dollars issued, Black Dollars outstanding, Black Dollars used, partner reimbursements owed and paid, partner dues received, ownership contributions received, service credits approved, mutual aid fund contributions and distributions, refunds, chargebacks, reversals, disputes, and adjustments.

Exports should include date, event type, amount, member ID, partner business ID, ledger account, status, payment processor reference, settlement reference, notes, and audit reference.

### 26.16 Financial Reporting Views

Admins and authorized finance operators should be able to view total STAR earned, total Black Dollars issued, outstanding, used, and reversed, total partner reimbursements pending and paid, total ownership contributions recognized, total dues allocated to ownership and mutual aid, total Mutual Aid / Community Development Fund balance, total partner dues collected, total disputed transactions, and total reversed transactions.

Member-facing views should be simpler and should not expose private financial details of other members or partners.

### 26.17 Ledger Integrity Controls

The system should enforce immutable event records, no direct balance editing, audit logs for all sensitive actions, role-based permissions, dual approval for high-risk adjustments, transaction IDs for every event, reversal-by-event instead of deletion, daily reconciliation, exportable reports, backup and recovery, and discrepancy alerts.

### 26.18 Daily Reconciliation

The system should run a daily reconciliation process to check calculated balances, outstanding Black Dollars, partner reimbursements, mutual aid fund display, ownership contribution balances, refund and chargeback allocations, dispute settlement protections, and pending event treatment. Any discrepancy should create an admin alert.

### 26.19 Section 26 Summary

The Simba Cooperative Economy must be ledger-first.

Every STAR point, Black Dollar, ownership contribution, fund allocation, partner reimbursement, dues allocation, reversal, dispute, and adjustment should be traceable to a recorded event.

The operating rule is:

**If it changes a balance, benefit, reimbursement, ownership pathway, or fund total, it must create a ledger event.**
